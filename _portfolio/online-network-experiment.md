We investigated the effectiveness of online social support and social comparison as strategies for improving sport participation by staging an 8-week network intervention in the online fitness and coaching platform [Final Surge](https://www.finalsurge.com/). The intervention was implemented among participants of a group-based beginner strength training course at the university sport center of a Dutch university. Groups are randomly assigned to one of three conditions: (1) a control condition, in which participants are asked to keep a log of their sports activities in the app; (2) a support condition, which supplements the control condition by activating a "Social Wall", where athletes’ activities are shared in the group, and where they can interact with each other based on their activities using comments and likes; and (3) a comparison condition, in which participants receive a weekly group leaderboard reflecting participants' training frequency and volume that week.

We developed an automated web tool to facilitate experimentation within the Final Surge application. Documentation can be found [here](https://fsurge.netlify.app/).
